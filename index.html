Natana Kasinathan

A renowned Indian historian, archaeologist, and epigraphist, Natana Kasinathan has left an indelible mark on Tamil Nadu’s rich heritage. As the former Director of the Tamil Nadu Archaeology Department, he played a pivotal role in uncovering and preserving ancient Tamil inscriptions, shining a light on early Tamil civilizations.

With over 105 books to his name, including notable works on Vanniyar history, and numerous prestigious accolades, his contributions continue to inspire and shape the study of history and archaeology in India.

About

Natana Kasinathan

Mr. Natana Kasinathan was born in 1940 in Chidambaram, Tamil Nadu, where he completed his early education. He pursued his undergraduate degree in BA History at Annamalai University in Chidambaram and later earned an MA in Archaeology from the University of Madras (now Chennai), further honing his expertise in the field.

He joined the Tamil Nadu Government Archaeology Department in 1967, where he dedicated his career to the field of archaeology. From 1989 to 1998, he served as the Director of the Tamil Nadu Archaeology Department, a period during which significant advancements were made in the exploration and preservation of Tamil heritage.

Under his leadership, many excavations were conducted across Tamil Nadu, shedding light on the state's rich historical and cultural past. One of the most notable achievements during his tenure was the underwater excavation of Poompuhar, which uncovered crucial evidence of ancient Tamil civilization. This groundbreaking project made a significant contribution to the study of underwater archaeology.

In addition to his archaeological work, Mr. Kasinathan is a prolific author, having written 105 books in both English and Tamil on various subjects related to Tamil history, archaeology, and epigraphy. His extensive research and writings have made a lasting impact on the field, and his works continue to be an invaluable resource for scholars and enthusiasts alike.

In 1984, Mr. Kasinathan played a pivotal role in organizing the 1000th-year commemoration of Raja Raja Chola’s rule, an important event that honored the legacy of one of Tamil Nadu's most renowned rulers.

During his directorship, he also contributed to the restoration and renovation of several important heritage sites, including the Tanjore Palace, Madurai Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace, and the Tharangambadi Fort, among others. These efforts were part of a wider initiative to preserve Tamil Nadu’s architectural and cultural heritage, particularly during the 8th World Tamil Conference in 1994. His work has left an indelible mark on the preservation of Tamil history and heritage, ensuring that future generations can access and appreciate these invaluable resources.

After his retirement, Mr. Natana Kasinathan established the Tamil Nadu Archaeology Institute and has since conducted nine historical conferences and published eight books containing research papers.

He has served as a former member of the Annamalai University Senate, a subject expert at the Central Institute of Indian Languages, a member of the expert committee for writing the history of Tamil Nadu, and as the chairman of the Vanniyar History Compilation Committee.

He has been honored with several prestigious titles. He was called “Kasunathan” by Dr. N. Sanjeevi, Professor of Tamil; "Kalvetta Semmal" by Editor Dr. Kulothungan of Kanmi Magazine; "Archaeological Scholar" by Dr. S. Meiyappan; "Kalvetta Arignar" by Epigraphist Dr. A. Mahadevan; “Expert in Inscription Studies” by Dr. R. Nagasamy; “Tamil Semmal” by Tamil Community Leader Dr. S. Ramadasu; “Historical Jewel” by Dr. V. Kanapathy Sthapathi; “Sculpture Guru” by the Vanniyar Sangam State Leader Maaveeran C. Guru; and “Historical Scholar” by the Thiruvaiyaru Tamilaiya Kalvi Kazhagam.

These recognitions reflect his extensive contribution to the field of archaeology and history, earning him widespread respect and admiration.
